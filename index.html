The World of Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are a aquatic animal that is split into 7 sub species. They can grow up to six feet long, and are able to live to be over 100 years old. Cheloniidae and Dermochelyidae families are the scientific classification for sea turtles. They are also very easily killed by straws somehow.

Sub Species

  1. Olive Ridley
  2. Green
  3. Flatback
  4. Hawksbill
  5. Leatherback
  6. Loggerhead
  7. Kemps Ridley

Habitat

Sea turtles live on ocean basins from tropical to sub-tropical climate. They spend most of their lives swimming, and can cover distances as far as Japan to California.

Diet

These amazing reptiles have a very diverse set of appitites by species. Some things that they enjoy can span from seagrasses, jellyfish, and even crabs.

Mateing Cycle

When sea turtles are around 7 to 13 years of age they are able to start mating. Several weeks before nesting season, courtship will occur causing impregnation. After that the turtle will bury her aggs on a shore somewhere. The baby sea turtles then hatch, and are completely on their own from day one.
